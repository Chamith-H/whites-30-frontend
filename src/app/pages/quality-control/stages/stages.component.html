Good Receipt Note Stage

The Goods Receipt Note (GRN) stage in SAP refers to the process of documenting the receipt of goods into a company's inventory from a supplier or vendor. It is a critical part of the Procure-to-Pay (P2P) cycle and ensures that the company acknowledges the receipt of goods ordered via a Purchase Order (PO).

Inventory Transfer Stage

The Inventory Transfer stage in SAP refers to the process of moving goods from one storage location or warehouse to another within the organization. This transaction ensures accurate stock updates across locations, supports internal logistics operations, and helps maintain real-time inventory visibility. It plays a key role in inventory management by reflecting physical stock movements within the system.

Production Receipt Stage

The Production Receipt stage in SAP refers to the process of recording finished goods received from production into inventory. This step confirms that manufactured items are complete and available for sale or further processing. It is a vital part of the production process, ensuring inventory accuracy, cost tracking, and real-time availability of finished goods in the system for subsequent use.

Finished Good Stage

The Finished Goods stage in SAP refers to the point where products have completed all manufacturing steps and are now ready for storage or delivery. This stage ensures that quality-checked goods are accurately classified, tracked, and moved to finished goods inventory, supporting sales and logistics.
